## 📚 Core Training Modules

### **Module 1: HIPAA Fundamentals (30 minutes)**
**Target:** All workforce members  
**Frequency:** Annual  
**Passing Score:** 85%

#### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
- Identify what constitutes PHI
- Recognize BioPoint's covered entity status
- Know individual rights under HIPAA

#### **Content Outline:**
1. **What is HIPAA?**
   - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
   - Privacy Rule vs. Security Rule
   - BioPoint's obligations as a covered entity

2. **Protected Health Information (PHI)**
   - Definition and examples
   - Identifiable information criteria
   - De-identification standards
   - Research data requirements (n≥50)

3. **Individual Rights**
   - Right to access
   - Right to request amendments
   - Right to accounting of disclosures
   - Right to request restrictions

4. **Penalties and Enforcement**
   - Civil penalties ($100 - $50,000 per violation)
   - Criminal penalties (up to $250,000 + 10 years prison)
   - OCR enforcement actions

#### **Assessment Questions:**
- Sample: "Which of the following is NOT considered PHI?"
- Sample: "What is the maximum civil penalty per HIPAA violation?"

---

### **Module 2: BioPoint-Specific PHI Handling (45 minutes)**
**Target:** All workforce members  
**Frequency:** Annual  
**Passing Score:** 90%

#### **Learning Objectives:**
- Identify PHI in BioPoint systems
- Understand data classification levels
- Apply minimum necessary principle
- Use appropriate access controls

#### **Content Outline:**
1. **BioPoint PHI Categories**
   - Lab reports and biomarkers
   - Progress photos (with health context)
   - User health tracking data
   - Supplement/medication stacks
   - Community health discussions

2. **System-Specific Guidelines**
   - Dashboard data handling
   - Lab upload procedures
   - Photo management protocols
   - Community content moderation
   - Research data aggregation

3. **Minimum Necessary Standard**
   - Access only what's needed for job function
   - Field-level restrictions
   - Purpose-based access
   - Documentation requirements

4. **Prohibited Actions**
   - No local PHI storage on personal devices
   - No unauthorized screenshots or downloads
   - No sharing with unauthorized personnel
   - No use for personal purposes

#### **Practical Scenarios:**
- "A user requests help accessing their lab data. What information can you access?"
- "You notice a bug that exposes other users' data. What do you do?"

3. **Device and Media Controls**

4. **Threat Recognition**

#### **Interactive Elements:**

---

### **Module 4: Incident Response (30 minutes)**
**Target:** All workforce members  
**Frequency:** Annual  
**Passing Score:** 95%

#### **Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize security incidents
- Follow immediate response procedures
- Understand notification requirements
- Cooperate with incident investigations

#### **Content Outline:**
1. **Incident Definition**
   - Unauthorized PHI access
   - System security breaches
   - Data loss or corruption
   - Policy violations

2. **Immediate Response**
   - Stop the activity (if possible)
   - Preserve evidence
   - Report immediately (within 2 hours)
   - Document observations

3. **Reporting Procedures**
   - Security Officer contact information
   - Incident hotline usage
   - Documentation requirements
   - Follow-up procedures

4. **Investigation Cooperation**
   - Interview participation
   - Evidence preservation
   - Confidentiality requirements
   - No retaliation policy

#### **Scenario-Based Assessment:**
- "You accidentally access another user's health data. What do you do?"
- "You suspect a coworker is inappropriately accessing PHI. What actions do you take?"

## 🎓 Role-Specific Training

### **Technical Staff Training (Additional 90 minutes)**
**Target:** Developers, DevOps, IT staff  
**Frequency:** Annual + quarterly updates  
**Prerequisites:** Core modules 1-4

#### **Technical Security Controls**
1. **Access Control Implementation**
   - Role-based access control (RBAC)
   - Principle of least privilege
   - Segregation of duties
   - Privileged access management

2. **Audit Logging Requirements**
   - Log all PHI access (CREATE, READ, UPDATE, DELETE)
   - Tamper-proof log storage
   - Log review procedures
   - Retention requirements (6 years)

3. **Encryption Standards**
   - Data at rest encryption (AES-256)
   - Data in transit encryption (TLS 1.3+)
   - Key management procedures
   - Certificate management

4. **Secure Development Practices**
   - Input validation and sanitization
   - SQL injection prevention
   - Cross-site scripting (XSS) prevention
   - Secure authentication implementation

- Security code review exercise
- Vulnerability identification lab
- Incident response drill

## 🚨 Special Training Situations

### **New Employee Onboarding**
- **Timeline:** Complete within first 5 business days
- **Content:** Core modules 1-4 + role-specific training
- **Assessment:** Must pass before system access granted
- **Mentoring:** Assigned security buddy for first month

### **Role Changes**
- **Trigger:** Any change in job responsibilities
- **Requirement:** Complete new role-specific training
- **Timeline:** Within 10 business days of change
- **Assessment:** Focus on new responsibilities

### **Incident-Triggered Training**
- **Trigger:** Security incident or policy violation
- **Scope:** Affected individuals or departments
- **Content:** Incident-specific remediation
- **Timeline:** Within 5 business days of incident

### **Regulatory Updates**
- **Trigger:** New HIPAA regulations or guidance
- **Scope:** All affected workforce members
- **Timeline:** Within 30 days of effective date
- **Method:** Update modules + supplementary training
