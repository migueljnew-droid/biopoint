## 📋 Executive Summary

This template provides a standardized Business Associate Agreement for BioPoint's relationships with vendors who may access, create, receive, maintain, or transmit protected health information (PHI) on our behalf.

**Legal Requirement:** 45 CFR 164.308(b) and 164.502(e)  
**OCR Compliance:** Based on HHS template and enforcement guidance  
**Risk Level:** High - Requires legal review before execution

## 🏢 Business Associate Agreement Template

### **BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT**

This Business Associate Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Effective Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**BioPoint, Inc.** ("Covered Entity"), a [State] corporation with its principal place of business at [Address], and

**[BUSINESS ASSOCIATE NAME]** ("Business Associate"), a [Entity Type] with its principal place of business at [Address].

Covered Entity and Business Associate are sometimes referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

### **ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS**

#### **1.1 HIPAA Definitions**
All terms used but not otherwise defined herein shall have the same meaning as those terms in the HIPAA Rules, including but not limited to:

- **"Breach"** shall have the same meaning as the term "breach" in 45 CFR 164.402
- **"Electronic Protected Health Information" (ePHI)** shall have the same meaning as the term "electronic protected health information" in 45 CFR 160.103
- **"HIPAA Rules"** shall mean the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164
- **"Protected Health Information" (PHI)** shall have the same meaning as the term "protected health information" in 45 CFR 160.103
- **"Required by Law"** shall have the same meaning as the term "required by law" in 45 CFR 164.103
- **"Secretary"** shall mean the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services
- **"Security Incident"** shall have the same meaning as the term "security incident" in 45 CFR 164.304
- **"Subcontractor"** shall have the same meaning as the term "subcontractor" in 45 CFR 160.103
- **"Unsecured Protected Health Information"** shall have the same meaning as the term "unsecured protected health information" in 45 CFR 164.402

#### **1.2 Additional Definitions**
- **"Agreement"** means this Business Associate Agreement
- **"Services"** means the services provided by Business Associate to Covered Entity as described in [Service Agreement/Statement of Work]
- **"Effective Date"** means [Effective Date]

### **ARTICLE 2: PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES**

#### **2.1 Permitted Uses**
Business Associate may only use PHI:

**A. For the Proper Management and Administration of Business Associate**
- Internal business operations related to Services
- Quality assurance and improvement activities
- Customer service and support
- Billing and collection activities

**B. To Carry Out Legal Responsibilities of Business Associate**
- Compliance with applicable federal and state laws
- Regulatory reporting requirements
- Judicial and administrative proceedings

**C. For Data Aggregation Purposes**
- Creating de-identified data sets
- Generating aggregate statistical reports
- Conducting comparative analyses
- Performance benchmarking

**D. For the Specific Services Outlined in [Service Agreement]**
- [Detailed description of specific services]
- [Specific data processing activities]
- [Authorized use cases]

#### **2.2 Permitted Disclosures**
Business Associate may only disclose PHI:

**A. To Subcontractors**
- Only for purposes permitted under this Agreement
- Only if Business Associate obtains satisfactory assurances in accordance with Article 7
- Only for functions that Business Associate has agreed to perform for Covered Entity

**B. To Carry Out Legal Responsibilities**
- As Required by Law (45 CFR 164.512(a))
- For public health activities (45 CFR 164.512(b))
- For health oversight activities (45 CFR 164.512(d))
- For judicial and administrative proceedings (45 CFR 164.512(e))

**C. For the Proper Management and Administration of Business Associate**
- Only if:
  1. The disclosure is Required by Law; OR
  2. Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances that the recipient will maintain the confidentiality of the PHI

#### **2.3 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures**
Business Associate shall NOT:
- Use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate the HIPAA Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity
- Use or disclose PHI except as permitted by this Agreement or Required by Law
- Use PHI for marketing purposes without specific authorization
- Sell PHI without specific authorization
- Use PHI for genetic information underwriting purposes

### **ARTICLE 3: SAFEGUARDS**

#### **3.1 Administrative Safeguards**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Security Officer Designation**
- Designate a Security Officer responsible for HIPAA compliance
- Provide contact information to Covered Entity
- Notify Covered Entity of any changes within 5 business days

**B. Workforce Training**
- Implement a security awareness and training program for all workforce members
- Provide role-specific training for workforce members with access to PHI
- Maintain training records for 6 years
- Conduct annual refresher training

**C. Access Management**
- Implement policies and procedures for authorizing access to PHI
- Establish criteria for granting access based on job responsibilities
- Implement procedures for terminating access
- Review access authorizations regularly (minimum annually)

**D. Security Incident Procedures**
- Implement policies and procedures to address security incidents
- Identify and respond to suspected or known security incidents
- Mitigate harmful effects of security incidents
- Document security incidents and their outcomes

#### **3.2 Physical Safeguards**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Workstation Security**
- Implement physical safeguards for all workstations that access PHI
- Restrict physical access to authorized personnel only
- Implement automatic screen locks (15 minutes maximum)
- Prohibit local storage of PHI on unsecured devices

**B. Device and Media Controls**
- Implement procedures for the final disposition of PHI
- Implement procedures for removal of PHI from electronic media
- Maintain documentation of all PHI disposal activities
- Use encryption for all PHI stored on portable devices

#### **3.3 Technical Safeguards**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Access Controls**
- Implement technical policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI
- Allow access only to those persons or software programs that have been granted access rights
- Implement automatic logoff procedures (15 minutes maximum)
- Implement encryption and decryption mechanisms

**B. Audit Controls**
- Implement hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms that record and examine activity in information systems that contain or use ePHI
- Review audit logs regularly (minimum weekly)
- Retain audit logs for 6 years
- Protect audit logs from tampering or unauthorized access

**C. Integrity**
- Implement policies and procedures to protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction
- Implement electronic mechanisms to corroborate that ePHI has not been altered or destroyed
- Implement data validation procedures
- Maintain backup and recovery procedures

**D. Transmission Security**
- Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI being transmitted over electronic communications networks
- Use encryption for all ePHI transmissions
- Implement integrity controls for all ePHI transmissions
- Use secure communication protocols (TLS 1.3 or higher)

### **ARTICLE 4: REPORTING AND NOTIFICATION**

#### **4.1 Security Incident Reporting**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Immediate Notification**
- Notify Covered Entity within 24 hours of discovery of any Security Incident
- Provide initial assessment of scope and impact
- Describe immediate containment measures taken

**B. Detailed Report**
- Provide detailed written report within 72 hours
- Include incident timeline, affected systems, and data involved
- Describe investigation findings and root cause analysis
- Document all remediation measures taken

**C. Ongoing Updates**
- Provide weekly status updates during active investigations
- Notify immediately of any material changes to incident scope
- Provide final report upon investigation completion

#### **4.2 Breach Notification**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Immediate Breach Notification**
- Notify Covered Entity immediately upon discovery of any potential Breach
- In no case later than 24 hours after discovery
- Provide all available details about the Breach

**B. Breach Assessment Support**
- Cooperate fully with Covered Entity's breach risk assessment
- Provide all information necessary for four-factor analysis
- Assist with individual and regulatory notifications as requested

**C. Documentation**
- Maintain complete documentation of all Breach-related activities
- Preserve all evidence related to the Breach
- Provide documentation to Covered Entity upon request

#### **4.3 Other Reporting**
Business Associate shall:

**A. Regulatory Requests**
- Immediately notify Covered Entity of any regulatory inquiries or audits
- Provide copies of all regulatory correspondence
- Cooperate with Covered Entity in responding to regulatory requests

**B. Law Enforcement Requests**
- Immediately notify Covered Entity of any law enforcement requests for PHI
- Obtain written documentation of legal authority for disclosure
- Provide copies of all law enforcement correspondence

---

### **ARTICLE 5: COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT RIGHTS**

#### **5.1 Compliance Certification**
Business Associate shall:
- Provide annual certification of HIPAA compliance
- Document all security measures implemented
- Provide evidence of workforce training completion
- Certify completion of risk assessments

#### **5.2 Audit Rights**
Covered Entity shall have the right to:
- Audit Business Associate's compliance with this Agreement
- Review policies, procedures, and documentation
- Interview workforce members regarding HIPAA compliance
- Inspect facilities and systems (with reasonable notice)

#### **5.3 Documentation Requirements**
Business Associate shall:
- Maintain all documentation required by HIPAA Rules
- Retain documentation for 6 years minimum
- Provide documentation to Covered Entity upon request
- Maintain documentation in readily accessible format

---

### **ARTICLE 6: TERM AND TERMINATION**

#### **6.1 Term**
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until terminated by either Party in accordance with this Article.

#### **6.2 Termination for Cause**
Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement immediately if:
- Business Associate violates any material provision of this Agreement
- Business Associate fails to cure any breach within 30 days of written notice
- Business Associate experiences a material security incident
- Business Associate becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy

#### **6.3 Effect of Termination**
Upon termination of this Agreement, Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI in its possession or control
- Provide certification of PHI destruction or return
- Retain no copies of PHI except as Required by Law
- Continue to protect any retained PHI in accordance with this Agreement

#### **6.4 Survival**
The obligations of Business Associate under Articles 3, 4, 5, and 6.3 shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

---

### **ARTICLE 7: SUBCONTRACTORS**

#### **7.1 Subcontractor Management**
Business Associate shall:
- Obtain satisfactory assurances from all subcontractors who will have access to PHI
- Ensure subcontractors agree to same restrictions and conditions as Business Associate
- Monitor subcontractor compliance with HIPAA requirements
- Terminate relationships with non-compliant subcontractors

#### **7.2 Subcontractor Agreements**
All subcontractor agreements shall:
- Include same or more stringent HIPAA protections as this Agreement
- Require immediate notification of any security incidents or breaches
- Provide audit rights to Covered Entity
- Include termination provisions for HIPAA violations

#### **7.3 Subcontractor Notification**
Business Associate shall:
- Maintain current list of all subcontractors with PHI access
- Notify Covered Entity of new subcontractors within 5 business days
- Provide copies of subcontractor agreements upon request
- Ensure subcontractors provide required certifications

---

### **ARTICLE 8: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION**

#### **8.1 Liability**
- Business Associate shall be liable for all acts and omissions of its workforce members and subcontractors
- Business Associate shall be liable for any HIPAA violations resulting from its failure to comply with this Agreement
- Each Party shall be responsible for its own attorney fees and costs unless otherwise specified

#### **8.2 Indemnification**
Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity from and against:
- Any claims arising from Business Associate's breach of this Agreement
- Any penalties imposed by OCR or other regulatory bodies
- Any damages resulting from Business Associate's negligence or willful misconduct
- Any third-party claims related to Business Associate's handling of PHI

---

### **ARTICLE 9: MISCELLANEOUS**

#### **9.1 Governing Law**
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [State] and applicable federal HIPAA regulations.

#### **9.2 Amendment**
This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both Parties. Any amendment must comply with current HIPAA requirements.

#### **9.3 Severability**
If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

#### **9.4 Entire Agreement**
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements.

#### **9.5 Counterparts**
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one instrument.
