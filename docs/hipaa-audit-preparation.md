## 🏛️ OCR Audit Process Overview

### Types of OCR Audits

**Random Compliance Audits:**
- Conducted without specific complaint or incident
- Typically focus on specific HIPAA requirements
- May be desk-based or on-site
- Usually completed within 30-60 days

**Complaint-Based Investigations:**
- Triggered by individual or third-party complaints
- Focus on specific alleged violations
- May include interviews and document review
- Timeline varies based on complexity

**Breach Investigations:**
- Initiated after breach notification to OCR
- Comprehensive review of incident handling
- Focus on breach response and notification compliance
- May result in corrective action or penalties

### Audit Selection Criteria

**Risk Factors for Selection:**
- Large covered entities with significant PHI volume
- History of complaints or breaches
- Business associates with access to multiple covered entities
- Emerging technology companies in healthcare
- Random selection for compliance monitoring

**BioPoint Risk Profile:**
- ✅ Medium risk profile (startup with < 500 employees)
- ✅ No history of OCR complaints
- ✅ No reported breaches to date
- ✅ Comprehensive compliance program implemented
- ✅ Recent implementation of all safeguards

### Audit Process Timeline

**Phase 1: Initial Contact (Days 1-7)**
- OCR notification letter received
- 10 business days to respond to information request
- Legal counsel engagement
- Internal team assembly
- Document preparation begins

**Phase 2: Document Review (Days 8-30)**
- Submit requested documentation
- Respond to follow-up questions
- Provide additional information as requested
- Coordinate with legal counsel
- Maintain detailed communication log

**Phase 3: On-Site Visit (If Required) (Days 31-45)**
- OCR auditor site visit
- Interviews with key personnel
- System demonstrations
- Physical security walkthrough
- Real-time question and answer sessions

**Phase 4: Resolution (Days 46-90)**
- OCR review and analysis
- Additional information requests
- Draft findings review
- Corrective action planning
- Final resolution and closure
