# ARTICLE I
# NAME, SITUS, AND GOVERNING LAW

## Section 1.1. Name.

This trust shall be known as the "OKLA DYNASTY TRUST" (hereinafter referred to as the "Trust").

## Section 1.2. Situs.

The situs of this Trust shall be the State of South Dakota. The Trustee shall maintain the principal place of administration of this Trust in the State of South Dakota, shall maintain trust records in South Dakota, and shall ensure that at least one Trustee or Co-Trustee is at all times a resident of South Dakota or a corporate trustee authorized to do business in South Dakota for purposes of establishing and maintaining South Dakota situs.

## Section 1.3. Governing Law.

(a) This Trust shall be governed by, construed, and administered in accordance with the laws of the State of South Dakota, including but not limited to the South Dakota Uniform Trust Code (SDCL Chapter 55) and the South Dakota Perpetual Trust Act.

(b) All questions concerning the validity, construction, and administration of this Trust shall be determined in accordance with South Dakota law.

(c) The Grantor expressly waives any application of the laws of any other jurisdiction to the administration of this Trust, except as may be required by applicable federal tax law.

## Section 1.4. Change of Situs.

(a) The Trust Protector, or in the absence of a Trust Protector, the Trustee, may change the situs of this Trust to another jurisdiction if:

(i) The laws of the current situs become unfavorable to the purposes of this Trust;

(ii) A change of situs would provide greater asset protection for beneficiaries;

(iii) A change of situs would provide more favorable tax treatment; or

(iv) Such change is otherwise in the best interests of the beneficiaries.

(b) Upon any change of situs, the governing law of this Trust shall automatically change to the laws of the new jurisdiction, unless otherwise directed by the Trust Protector.

## Section 1.5. Flee Clause.

(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Trust, if at any time:

(i) Any court, tribunal, or governmental authority of any jurisdiction (other than South Dakota) attempts to exercise jurisdiction over this Trust or any Trustee;

(ii) Any creditor or potential creditor of any beneficiary commences legal proceedings in any jurisdiction (other than South Dakota) to attach, levy upon, or otherwise reach Trust assets;

(iii) Any court enters an order purporting to require the Trustee to make distributions from this Trust contrary to its terms;

(iv) The laws of South Dakota change in a manner materially adverse to the asset protection purposes of this Trust; or

(v) Any event occurs that, in the sole judgment of the Trust Protector or Trustee, threatens the integrity, protection, or perpetual nature of this Trust;

Then, automatically and without the need for any action by any person:

(A) The situs of this Trust shall immediately and automatically move to another jurisdiction selected by the Trust Protector (or if no Trust Protector exists, by the Trustee) that provides maximum asset protection and perpetual duration;

(B) A successor Trustee located in such jurisdiction shall automatically be appointed;

(C) All Trust assets shall be immediately transferred to such successor Trustee;

(D) The governing law of this Trust shall change to the laws of such new jurisdiction; and

(E) The former Trustee shall be released from all further duties and obligations.

(b) The Trustee shall maintain a list of alternative jurisdictions and successor trustees for purposes of this Flee Clause.

# ARTICLE IV
# DURATION

## Section 4.1. Perpetual Duration.

(a) This Trust shall continue in perpetuity. The Grantor expressly invokes the South Dakota Perpetual Trust Act and declares that this Trust is not subject to any rule against perpetuities, rule limiting the duration of trusts, or similar rule of any jurisdiction.

(b) This Trust shall continue for the benefit of successive generations of beneficiaries until terminated in accordance with Article XIV of this Trust Agreement.

## Section 4.2. No Rule Against Perpetuities.

(a) The Grantor intends that this Trust shall not be subject to any common law or statutory rule against perpetuities.

(b) To the extent any rule against perpetuities or similar rule is applied to this Trust, the Trust shall terminate twenty-one (21) years after the death of the last survivor of the beneficiaries living on the date of this Trust Agreement, and the Trustee shall thereupon distribute the Trust Estate as provided in Article XIV.

(c) The Grantor expressly elects application of South Dakota law for purposes of perpetuities, and specifically SDCL 43-5-8, which abolishes the rule against perpetuities for trusts governed by South Dakota law.

## Section 4.3. Dynasty Purpose.

The Grantor's intent is that this Trust shall preserve and grow the Trust Estate for the benefit of the Grantor's descendants for as many generations as legally permissible, ideally in perpetuity. The Trustee and Trust Protector shall interpret and administer this Trust in accordance with this dynasty purpose.

# ARTICLE VI
# DISTRIBUTIONS

## Section 6.1. Wholly Discretionary Distributions.

(a) **Absolute Discretion.** The Trustee shall have sole, absolute, and uncontrolled discretion to distribute to or for the benefit of any one or more of the Current Beneficiaries so much of the net income and principal of the Trust Estate, up to the whole thereof, as the Trustee, in the Trustee's sole and absolute discretion, may from time to time determine.

(b) **No Required Distribution Standard.** The Trustee is not required to consider any beneficiary's other resources, income, or assets in making distribution decisions. The Trustee may make distributions to one beneficiary to the complete exclusion of other beneficiaries.

(c) **No Ascertainable Standard.** The distribution standard under this Trust is wholly discretionary and is NOT an ascertainable standard such as health, education, maintenance, and support ("HEMS"). The Trustee may make distributions for any purpose whatsoever or for no purpose at all.

(d) **Unequal Distributions.** The Trustee may make distributions unequally among beneficiaries and may favor one or more beneficiaries to the complete exclusion of others.

(e) **Accumulation.** Any income not distributed may be accumulated and added to principal.

## Section 6.2. Factors the Trustee May Consider.

In exercising discretion regarding distributions, the Trustee MAY, but is not required to, consider any of the following factors:

(a) The beneficiary's current and anticipated future needs;

(b) The beneficiary's age, health, and life expectancy;

(c) The beneficiary's education, skills, and earning capacity;

(d) The beneficiary's other resources and income;

(e) The beneficiary's lifestyle and standard of living;

(f) The beneficiary's financial responsibility and maturity;

(g) The effect of distributions on any governmental benefits;

(h) Tax considerations;

(i) The preservation of the Trust Estate for future generations;

(j) Any other factor the Trustee deems relevant.

## Section 6.3. Distributions for Minors.

(a) If any beneficiary is a minor, the Trustee may make distributions for such beneficiary's benefit by any of the following methods:

(i) Direct payment to the beneficiary;

(ii) Payment to the beneficiary's parent or legal guardian;

(iii) Payment to the beneficiary's custodian under any Uniform Transfers to Minors Act;

(iv) Payment directly to third parties for goods and services provided to the beneficiary;

(v) Establishment of a custodial account for the beneficiary; or

(vi) Any other method the Trustee deems appropriate.

(b) The Trustee shall have no duty to see to the application of any distribution made pursuant to this Section.

## Section 6.4. Distributions for Incapacitated Beneficiaries.

If any beneficiary is incapacitated (as determined by the Trustee), the Trustee may make distributions for such beneficiary's benefit in any manner the Trustee deems appropriate, including payment to a guardian, conservator, agent under a power of attorney, or directly to third parties for goods and services.

## Section 6.5. No Requirement to Distribute.

The Trustee is under no obligation to make any distribution from this Trust. The beneficiaries have no enforceable right to compel distributions. The Trustee may accumulate all income and retain all principal if the Trustee determines, in the Trustee's sole discretion, that accumulation is in the best interests of the current and future beneficiaries.

## Section 6.6. Tax-Sensitive Distributions.

(a) The Trustee may, but is not required to, consider the tax consequences of any distribution, including income tax, gift tax, estate tax, and generation-skipping transfer tax consequences.

(b) The Trustee may make distributions to take advantage of lower marginal tax brackets of beneficiaries.

(c) The Trustee shall exercise care to preserve any exemptions allocated to this Trust under the generation-skipping transfer tax provisions of the Internal Revenue Code.

## Section 6.7. Loans to Beneficiaries.

(a) The Trustee, in the Trustee's discretion, may make loans to any beneficiary on such terms and conditions as the Trustee deems appropriate.

(b) Any loan shall bear interest at a rate not less than the applicable federal rate under Internal Revenue Code Section 7520 at the time the loan is made.

(c) The Trustee may, in the Trustee's discretion, forgive all or any portion of a loan to a beneficiary.

# ARTICLE VII
# SPENDTHRIFT PROVISIONS

## Section 7.1. Spendthrift Protection.

(a) **Anti-Alienation.** No beneficiary shall have any right, power, or authority to sell, assign, transfer, pledge, mortgage, hypothecate, encumber, anticipate, or in any other manner alienate or dispose of all or any part of such beneficiary's interest in the income or principal of this Trust prior to actual receipt thereof by such beneficiary.

(b) **Anti-Attachment.** No interest of any beneficiary in the income or principal of this Trust shall be subject to any claims of any creditor of such beneficiary, or to any legal process, bankruptcy proceedings, garnishment, attachment, execution, levy, or any other form of involuntary transfer, prior to actual receipt thereof by such beneficiary.

(c) **Absolute Protection.** The spendthrift protections of this Section shall be absolute and shall apply to all beneficiaries, whether or not they are also trustees, trust protectors, or powerholders under this Trust.

## Section 7.2. Protection from Voluntary and Involuntary Transfer.

(a) No beneficiary may voluntarily or involuntarily transfer, assign, or encumber any interest in this Trust.

(b) No interest of any beneficiary in this Trust shall be subject to claims of alimony, child support, or other domestic obligations (except to the extent required by applicable law).

(c) No court shall have the power to order the Trustee to make any distribution from this Trust to or for the benefit of any creditor of any beneficiary.

## Section 7.3. Protection from Beneficiary's Creditors.

To the maximum extent permitted by South Dakota law and the South Dakota Uniform Trust Code:

(a) No creditor or assignee of any beneficiary shall have any right or power to reach any interest of such beneficiary in the Trust, or any distribution from the Trust prior to actual receipt by the beneficiary.

(b) The spendthrift provisions of this Trust shall be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted by South Dakota law (SDCL 55-1-36 et seq.).

(c) This Trust is intended to qualify for the maximum asset protection available under South Dakota law, including protection from tort creditors, contract creditors, divorce judgments, and all other claims.

## Section 7.4. Discretionary Trust Nature.

This Trust is a discretionary trust, not a support trust. No beneficiary has any enforceable right to any distribution. The Trustee's discretion is absolute and unreviewable. This discretionary nature provides additional protection beyond the spendthrift provisions.

## Section 7.5. Exception Creditors.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, to the extent required by applicable law, the spendthrift provisions of this Trust shall not protect a beneficiary's interest from:

(a) Child support obligations that are reduced to judgment;

(b) Claims by the United States or the State of South Dakota; or

(c) Such other claims as may be excepted from spendthrift protection under SDCL 55-1-36 et seq.

## Section 7.6. Reformation.

If any court finds that the spendthrift provisions of this Trust are unenforceable against any creditor, the Trustee shall have the power to distribute such beneficiary's share to a separate trust for such beneficiary's benefit with terms that provide maximum protection from such creditor, or to suspend distributions to such beneficiary until such claim is resolved.

# ARTICLE VIII
# DOMESTIC ASSET PROTECTION TRUST PROVISIONS

## Section 8.1. South Dakota Asset Protection Trust.

This Trust is intended to qualify as a Domestic Asset Protection Trust under South Dakota law (SDCL Chapter 55-16) and the South Dakota Qualified Disposition in Trust Act.

## Section 8.2. Qualified Disposition.

Each transfer of property to this Trust by the Grantor or any other transferor shall constitute a "qualified disposition" under SDCL 55-16-2.

## Section 8.3. Asset Protection Features.

(a) **Discretionary Trust.** This Trust is a wholly discretionary trust. No beneficiary has any enforceable right to compel distributions.

(b) **Spendthrift Clause.** This Trust contains a spendthrift clause that is enforceable under South Dakota law.

(c) **South Dakota Trustee.** The Trustee shall ensure that at least one trustee is a qualified person under South Dakota law, meaning an individual who is a resident of South Dakota or a corporation or entity authorized to act as trustee in South Dakota.

(d) **South Dakota Administration.** Some or all of the administration of this Trust shall occur in South Dakota, including maintaining trust records, preparing or arranging for tax returns, and materially participating in the administration of the Trust.

## Section 8.4. Exclusion of Transferor as Beneficiary.

(a) The Grantor is not and shall not be a beneficiary of this Trust.

(b) Any other person who transfers property to this Trust shall not be a beneficiary of this Trust with respect to the property so transferred, unless such person is already a beneficiary under the terms of this Trust.

## Section 8.5. Fraudulent Transfer Protection.

(a) The Grantor represents and warrants that:

(i) The Grantor is not transferring property to this Trust with the intent to defraud any creditor;

(ii) The Grantor is solvent at the time of each transfer and will remain solvent after each transfer;

(iii) The transfer does not render the Grantor insolvent;

(iv) The Grantor is not contemplating filing for bankruptcy;

(v) The Grantor does not have any pending or threatened litigation that would give rise to a creditor claim; and

(vi) The Grantor is transferring property to this Trust for legitimate estate planning purposes.

(b) The Trustee may require an affidavit from any person transferring property to this Trust confirming compliance with this Section.

## Section 8.6. Limitations Period.

Under South Dakota law (SDCL 55-16-9), a creditor may not bring an action to set aside a transfer to this Trust unless:

(a) The creditor's claim arose before the transfer and the action is brought within the later of two (2) years after the transfer or six (6) months after the creditor discovers or reasonably should have discovered the transfer; or

(b) The creditor's claim arose after the transfer and the action is brought within two (2) years after the transfer.

## Section 8.7. Burden of Proof.

Any creditor challenging a transfer to this Trust shall bear the burden of proving by clear and convincing evidence that the transfer was made with actual intent to defraud that creditor.

The Trustee shall not be liable for:

(a) Any decline in the value of Trust investments;

(b) Any action taken or omitted in good faith;

(c) Following the direction of the Trust Protector when required or permitted by this Trust;

(d) Failing to diversify Trust investments if the Trustee reasonably believes retention of the investment is in the best interests of the beneficiaries; or

(e) Any other matter except willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breach of fiduciary duty.

## Section 9.10. Indemnification.

The Trustee shall be indemnified from the Trust Estate for any liability, loss, damage, expense, or cost (including reasonable attorneys' fees) incurred by the Trustee in connection with the administration of this Trust, except to the extent caused by the Trustee's willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breach of fiduciary duty.

No information about this Trust shall be provided directly to any beneficiary under the age of twenty-five (25) years, unless the Trust Protector directs otherwise. The Trustee may provide such information to the beneficiary's parent or guardian or to a designated representative.

## Section 12.4. Release of Duty to Inform.

To the extent permitted by South Dakota law, the Trust Protector is hereby authorized to release the Trustee from any duty to inform beneficiaries and to account to beneficiaries as may otherwise be required by law.

# ARTICLE XIII
# POWERHOLDER PROVISIONS

## Section 13.1. Limited Power of Appointment.

(a) Upon the death of each Primary Beneficiary, such Primary Beneficiary shall have a limited testamentary power of appointment to appoint all or any part of such Primary Beneficiary's share of the Trust Estate, exercisable by specific reference to this power in the Primary Beneficiary's last will and testament, to or among one or more of the following:

(i) The descendants of the Grantor (other than the deceased Primary Beneficiary, the deceased Primary Beneficiary's estate, the deceased Primary Beneficiary's creditors, or the creditors of the deceased Primary Beneficiary's estate); and

(ii) Trusts for the benefit of such descendants.

(b) This power of appointment is a limited power and may not be exercised in favor of the powerholder, the powerholder's estate, the powerholder's creditors, or the creditors of the powerholder's estate.

## Section 13.2. Default Distribution.

(a) To the extent a Primary Beneficiary does not effectively exercise the limited power of appointment, such Primary Beneficiary's share shall be distributed to such Primary Beneficiary's then-living descendants, per stirpes.

(b) If a Primary Beneficiary dies without living descendants, such Primary Beneficiary's share shall be distributed to the remaining Primary Beneficiaries and their descendants, per stirpes.

(c) If no Primary Beneficiaries or their descendants are living, the Trust Estate shall be distributed to the Contingent Beneficiaries as provided in Section 5.3.

## Section 13.3. Continuing Trust.

Any distribution to a descendant of a Primary Beneficiary under this Article shall be held in a separate trust for such descendant under the same terms and conditions as this Trust, unless the Trustee determines that distribution outright is in the best interests of such descendant and consistent with the dynasty purposes of this Trust.

# ARTICLE XIV
# TERMINATION

## Section 14.1. Termination Events.

This Trust shall terminate upon the earliest of:

(a) The distribution of all Trust assets in accordance with the terms of this Trust;

(b) A determination by the Trustee and Trust Protector that continued administration of the Trust is no longer economically feasible; or

(c) A termination ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.

## Section 14.2. Final Distribution.

Upon termination of this Trust, the Trustee shall distribute the remaining Trust Estate to the then-Current Beneficiaries, per stirpes, or if there are no Current Beneficiaries, to the Contingent Beneficiaries.

## Section 14.3. Small Trust Termination.

If the aggregate fair market value of the Trust Estate falls below Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($500,000), the Trustee may, in the Trustee's discretion, terminate this Trust and distribute the Trust Estate to the then-Current Beneficiaries.

(viii) Takes any other action that is adverse to this Trust or its purposes;

Then such beneficiary shall be treated as having predeceased the Grantor for all purposes of this Trust, and all provisions in favor of such beneficiary shall be void and of no effect.

## Section 15.2. Good Cause Exception.

This no contest clause shall not apply to:

(a) Any action brought in good faith to interpret the terms of this Trust;

(b) Any action brought in good faith to enforce the terms of this Trust;

(c) Any action brought in good faith to hold a Trustee accountable for breach of fiduciary duty; or

(d) Any action required by law.

## Section 15.3. Enforcement.

The Trustee is authorized and directed to enforce this no contest clause and to defend any contest, with all expenses of such defense paid from the Trust Estate.

# GENERATION-SKIPPING TRANSFER TAX PROVISIONS

## Section 16.1. GST Tax Exempt Trust.

(a) The Grantor intends to allocate all or a portion of the Grantor's generation-skipping transfer tax exemption to transfers to this Trust.

(b) The Trustee shall administer this Trust in a manner consistent with maintaining its status as a generation-skipping transfer tax exempt trust.

## Section 16.2. Division Into Exempt and Non-Exempt Trusts.

If the Grantor's generation-skipping transfer tax exemption is not sufficient to render this entire Trust exempt from generation-skipping transfer tax, the Trustee shall divide this Trust into two separate trusts:

(a) An exempt trust having an inclusion ratio of zero; and

(b) A non-exempt trust having an inclusion ratio of one.

## Section 16.3. Administration of Exempt Trust.

The exempt trust shall be administered so as to maximize the benefits of the generation-skipping transfer tax exemption, including by making distributions primarily to skip persons (grandchildren and more remote descendants).

## Section 16.4. Prohibition on Taxable Distributions.

The Trustee shall not make any distribution that would result in a taxable distribution or taxable termination under Chapter 13 of the Internal Revenue Code, unless the Trustee determines in the Trustee's discretion that such distribution is in the best interests of the beneficiaries notwithstanding the tax consequences.

## Section 16.5. GST Savings Clause.

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Trust, no Trustee, Trust Protector, or beneficiary shall have any power that would cause:

(a) This Trust to lose its status as a generation-skipping transfer tax exempt trust;

(b) Any portion of the Trust Estate to be includible in the estate of any beneficiary for federal estate tax purposes; or

(c) Any power to be treated as a general power of appointment under Internal Revenue Code Section 2041 or 2514.
