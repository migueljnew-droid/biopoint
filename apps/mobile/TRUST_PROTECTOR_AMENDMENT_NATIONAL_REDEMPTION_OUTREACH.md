**(c) Trust Protector Committee Option.** In recognition of the ecclesiastical governance of the Assembly, a Trust Protector Committee may be designated in lieu of a single Trust Protector, consisting of not fewer than three (3) and not more than seven (7) individuals. If a Trust Protector Committee is designated:
   - (i) Members should, where practicable, include individuals with demonstrated commitment to the spiritual mission and ministerial objectives of National Redemption Outreach;
   - (ii) All powers granted herein shall be exercised by majority vote of the Committee members then serving;

**(d) Vacancy.** If no Trust Protector or Successor Trust Protector is serving or willing to serve:
   - (i) The elders, ministers, or authorized governing body of the Assembly may appoint a successor Trust Protector by majority written consent; or

**(e) Qualifications.** The Trust Protector should be an individual or entity with:
   - (i) Understanding of and commitment to the religious mission and spiritual objectives of National Redemption Outreach;
   - (ii) No adverse interest to the Trust, its beneficiaries, or the Assembly's mission;
   - (iii) Ability to fulfill fiduciary obligations;
   - (iv) Where practicable, experience with religious organization governance or nonprofit administration.

### Section 2. Powers of Trust Protector

The Trust Protector shall have the following powers, to be exercised in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of the Trust, the Assembly, its ministry, and all beneficiaries:

**(a) Removal and Replacement of Trustee.** The Trust Protector may remove any Trustee, including any corporate Trustee such as ARX INFINITUM or its successors, with or without cause, and appoint a successor Trustee. In exercising this power, the Trust Protector shall consider the best interests of the Trust, the continuity of its religious mission, and the spiritual welfare of the Assembly.

**(b) Appointment of Additional or Successor Trustees.** The Trust Protector may appoint successor Trustees or additional Co-Trustees as the Trust Protector deems appropriate for the proper administration of the Trust and advancement of its religious purposes.

**(c) Modification of Administrative Provisions.** The Trust Protector may modify, amend, or supplement any administrative provisions of the Trust, provided that:
   - (i) Such modifications shall not alter the fundamental dispositive provisions or beneficial interests except as otherwise provided herein;
   - (ii) Such modifications shall be consistent with the religious and ministerial objectives of the Trust;
   - (iii) Such modifications shall not violate the Assembly's ecclesiastical principles or doctrinal positions.

**(d) Change of Situs and Governing Law.** The Trust Protector may change the situs (principal place of administration) of the Trust and the governing law applicable to the Trust to any jurisdiction the Trust Protector deems to be in the best interests of the Trust, its beneficiaries, and the preservation of its religious freedom and ministerial mission.

**(e) Decanting Authority.** The Trust Protector may distribute or appoint all or any portion of the Trust assets to a new trust (a "Recipient Trust") with terms substantially similar to those of this Trust, provided that:
   - (i) Such decanting does not reduce the interests of any current beneficiary;
   - (ii) The Recipient Trust maintains the religious and ministerial purposes of this Trust;
   - (iii) Such decanting does not violate any express restriction in this Trust Agreement;
   - (iv) The Recipient Trust qualifies for tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or applicable provisions.

**(f) Addition or Removal of Beneficiaries.** The Trust Protector may add or remove beneficiaries from the class of eligible beneficiaries, provided that:
   - (i) Any added beneficiary shall be a member of the Assembly, a minister or servant of the Assembly, a descendant or family member of a member, an affiliated religious organization, or a charitable organization aligned with the Trust's religious mission;
   - (ii) Removal of a beneficiary shall only be for good cause, including but not limited to: threats of litigation against the Trust, creditor issues affecting Trust assets, conduct contrary to the religious principles of the Assembly, or conduct detrimental to the Trust or its mission.

**(g) Veto of Distributions.** The Trust Protector may veto any proposed distribution by the Trustee prior to such distribution being made. The Trustee shall provide reasonable notice to the Trust Protector of any intended discretionary distribution exceeding $_____________ [amount to be specified] or as otherwise directed by the Trust Protector.

**(h) Grantor/Non-Grantor Trust Conversion.** The Trust Protector may convert the Trust between grantor trust status and non-grantor trust status under the Internal Revenue Code, as the Trust Protector determines to be advantageous for the Trust, its tax-exempt status, and its beneficiaries.

**(i) Amendment for Law Changes.** The Trust Protector may amend the Trust to take advantage of changes in applicable tax law, trust law, nonprofit law, religious organization law, or other relevant statutes or regulations, provided such amendments are consistent with the original intent of the Trust as expressed in the Original Trust Agreement and preserve the religious mission and tax-exempt status.

**(j) Termination Authority.** The Trust Protector may terminate the Trust if, in the Trust Protector's sole discretion, the continuation of the Trust has become impractical, uneconomical, or contrary to the best interests of the beneficiaries and the religious mission. Upon termination:
   - (i) Trust assets shall be distributed in accordance with the dispositive provisions of the Original Trust Agreement;
   - (ii) If those provisions are inapplicable, assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations with religious or charitable purposes similar to those of National Redemption Outreach, qualifying under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

**(k) Dispute Resolution.** The Trust Protector shall have the power to resolve disputes between Trustees and beneficiaries, among beneficiaries, or between the Trust and ecclesiastical governance bodies, concerning the interpretation or administration of the Trust. The Trust Protector's decision on such matters shall be final and binding on all parties.

**(l) Protection of Religious Freedom and Ministry.** The Trust Protector may take such actions as are necessary to:
   - (i) Protect the religious freedom, ministerial activities, and ecclesiastical autonomy of National Redemption Outreach;
   - (ii) Preserve sacred property, religious artifacts, ministerial records, and spiritual heritage held within or associated with the Trust;
   - (iii) Defend the Trust against governmental overreach or interference with religious practice.

**(m) Preservation of Tax-Exempt Status.** The Trust Protector shall have the power to take any action necessary to preserve, protect, or restore the tax-exempt status of the Trust under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or applicable state provisions.

### Section 3. Limitations on Trust Protector Powers

**(a) No Self-Benefit.** The Trust Protector shall not exercise any power in a manner that would result in a direct or indirect financial benefit to the Trust Protector personally, except that a Trust Protector who is also a beneficiary may receive distributions otherwise authorized under the Trust.

**(b) No Revocation.** The Trust Protector shall not have the power to revoke this Trust or to make this Trust revocable.

**(c) Protection of Primary Beneficiaries and Religious Mission.** The Trust Protector shall not:
   - (i) Change the identity of the primary beneficiaries or substantially reduce their interests without good cause;
   - (ii) Alter the fundamental religious and ministerial mission of the Trust;
   - (iii) Take any action that would cause the Trust to fail to qualify for tax-exempt status;
   - (iv) Dispose of sacred property or religious artifacts without consideration of their spiritual significance to the Assembly.

**(d) Fiduciary Duty.** The Trust Protector shall act as a fiduciary and shall exercise all powers in good faith and in the best interests of the Trust, its beneficiaries, and the religious mission of the Assembly.

**(e) Good Faith Requirement.** All actions taken by the Trust Protector shall be taken in good faith and with reasonable care.

**(f) Compliance with Ecclesiastical Governance.** Where applicable, the Trust Protector shall exercise powers in a manner consistent with any applicable ecclesiastical governance requirements, doctrinal positions, or religious principles of National Redemption Outreach.

**(g) Preservation of Religious Character.** The Trust Protector shall not take any action that would fundamentally alter the religious character or spiritual mission of National Redemption Outreach.

### Section 4. Resignation and Succession

**(a) Resignation.** A Trust Protector may resign by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the Trustee and, if reasonably practicable, to the adult beneficiaries and any ecclesiastical governance body.

**(b) Incapacity.** A Trust Protector shall be deemed to have resigned upon becoming legally incapacitated as determined by a court of competent jurisdiction or by written certification of two (2) licensed physicians.

**(c) Removal of Trust Protector.** A Trust Protector may be removed for cause (including breach of fiduciary duty, incapacity, conduct contrary to the religious mission, or willful misconduct) by:
   - (i) Unanimous written consent of the adult beneficiaries; or
   - (ii) Majority vote of the elders, ministers, or authorized ecclesiastical governance body; or
   - (iii) Order of a court of competent jurisdiction.

**(d) Successor Appointment.** Upon resignation, removal, incapacity, or death of the Trust Protector, the Successor Trust Protector shall serve. If no Successor Trust Protector is designated or willing to serve, a successor may be appointed as provided in Section 1(d).

### Section 5. Compensation and Indemnification

**(a) Compensation.** The Trust Protector shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, payable from the Trust assets, in an amount comparable to fees charged by professional fiduciaries for similar services in the relevant jurisdiction. Alternatively, the Trust Protector may serve without compensation as a ministry of service.

**(b) Indemnification.** The Trust Protector shall be indemnified and held harmless from the Trust assets for any liability, cost, or expense incurred in the good faith performance of duties hereunder, except for liability arising from willful misconduct, gross negligence, or bad faith.

**(c) Advancement of Expenses.** The Trust may advance expenses to the Trust Protector for legal defense or other matters arising from the Trust Protector's service, subject to repayment if it is ultimately determined that the Trust Protector acted in bad faith or with willful misconduct.

### Section 6. Exercise of Powers

**(a) Written Instrument.** Any power exercised by the Trust Protector shall be exercised by written instrument delivered to the Trustee and, where appropriate, recorded or filed as required by law.

**(b) No Duty to Act.** The Trust Protector shall have no duty to monitor the Trustee or the Trust and shall have no liability for failing to exercise any power granted herein.

**(c) Discretionary Nature.** All powers granted to the Trust Protector are discretionary, and the Trust Protector's decision to exercise or not exercise any power shall not be subject to review or challenge, except for actions taken in bad faith.

**(d) Consultation.** The Trust Protector may, but shall not be required to, consult with beneficiaries, ministers, elders, or other advisors before exercising any power.

**(e) Prayerful Consideration.** The Trust Protector is encouraged to exercise all powers with prayerful consideration and in accordance with the spiritual principles of the Assembly.

### Section 7. Relationship to Ecclesiastical Governance

**(a) Coordination.** The Trust Protector shall coordinate with any applicable ecclesiastical governance structure in the administration of Trust Protector duties, while maintaining independent fiduciary judgment.

**(b) Preservation of Mission.** The Trust Protector shall at all times endeavor to preserve and advance the religious, ministerial, educational, and charitable mission of National Redemption Outreach.

**(c) Ecclesiastical Matters.** Matters of doctrine, religious practice, ministerial appointment, and ecclesiastical discipline shall remain within the authority of the appropriate ecclesiastical governance bodies and shall not be subject to the Trust Protector's powers, except as such matters directly affect the administration or protection of Trust assets.

### Section 8. Tax-Exempt Status Provisions

**(a) 501(c)(3) Compliance.** Notwithstanding any other provision of this Amendment, the Trust Protector shall not take any action that would cause the Trust to fail to qualify as an organization described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

**(b) Private Inurement.** No part of the net earnings of the Trust shall inure to the benefit of any private individual, and the Trust Protector shall ensure that no exercise of power results in such private inurement.

**(c) Political Activities.** The Trust Protector shall not direct or permit the Trust to participate in or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.
