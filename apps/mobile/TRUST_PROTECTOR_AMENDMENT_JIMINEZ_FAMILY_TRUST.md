**(c) Trust Protector Committee Option.** In lieu of a single Trust Protector, the Grantor may designate a Trust Protector Committee consisting of not fewer than two (2) and not more than five (5) individuals. If a Trust Protector Committee is designated, all powers granted herein shall be exercised by majority vote of the Committee members then serving, unless otherwise specified.

**(d) Vacancy.** If no Trust Protector or Successor Trust Protector is serving or willing to serve, the adult beneficiaries holding a majority interest in the Trust may appoint a successor Trust Protector by unanimous written consent.

### Section 2. Powers of Trust Protector

The Trust Protector shall have the following powers, to be exercised in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of the Trust and its beneficiaries:

**(a) Removal and Replacement of Trustee.** The Trust Protector may remove any Trustee, including any corporate Trustee such as ARX INFINITUM or its successors, with or without cause, and appoint a successor Trustee.

**(b) Appointment of Additional or Successor Trustees.** The Trust Protector may appoint successor Trustees or additional Co-Trustees as the Trust Protector deems appropriate for the proper administration of the Trust.

**(c) Modification of Administrative Provisions.** The Trust Protector may modify, amend, or supplement any administrative provisions of the Trust, provided that such modifications shall not alter the dispositive provisions or beneficial interests except as otherwise provided herein.

**(d) Change of Situs and Governing Law.** The Trust Protector may change the situs (principal place of administration) of the Trust and the governing law applicable to the Trust to any jurisdiction the Trust Protector deems to be in the best interests of the Trust and its beneficiaries.

**(e) Decanting Authority.** The Trust Protector may distribute or appoint all or any portion of the Trust assets to a new trust (a "Recipient Trust") with terms substantially similar to those of this Trust, provided that such decanting does not reduce the interests of any current beneficiary or violate any express restriction in this Trust Agreement.

**(f) Addition or Removal of Beneficiaries.** The Trust Protector may add or remove beneficiaries from the class of eligible beneficiaries, provided that:
   - (i) Any added beneficiary shall be a lineal descendant of the Grantor, a spouse of a lineal descendant, or a charitable organization;
   - (ii) Removal of a beneficiary shall only be for good cause, including but not limited to: threats of litigation, creditor issues, divorce proceedings, or conduct detrimental to the Trust or other beneficiaries.

**(g) Veto of Distributions.** The Trust Protector may veto any proposed distribution by the Trustee prior to such distribution being made. The Trustee shall provide reasonable notice to the Trust Protector of any intended discretionary distribution exceeding $_____________ [amount to be specified] or as otherwise directed by the Trust Protector.

**(h) Grantor/Non-Grantor Trust Conversion.** The Trust Protector may convert the Trust between grantor trust status and non-grantor trust status under the Internal Revenue Code, as the Trust Protector determines to be advantageous for the Trust and its beneficiaries.

**(i) Amendment for Law Changes.** The Trust Protector may amend the Trust to take advantage of changes in applicable tax law, trust law, or other relevant statutes or regulations, provided such amendments are consistent with the Grantor's original intent as expressed in the Original Trust Agreement.

**(j) Termination Authority.** The Trust Protector may terminate the Trust if, in the Trust Protector's sole discretion, the continuation of the Trust has become impractical, uneconomical, or contrary to the best interests of the beneficiaries. Upon termination, Trust assets shall be distributed in accordance with the dispositive provisions of the Original Trust Agreement or, if those provisions are inapplicable, to the then-current beneficiaries in proportion to their respective interests.

**(k) Dispute Resolution.** The Trust Protector shall have the power to resolve disputes between Trustees and beneficiaries, and between or among beneficiaries, concerning the interpretation or administration of the Trust. The Trust Protector's decision on such matters shall be final and binding on all parties.

### Section 3. Limitations on Trust Protector Powers

**(a) No Self-Benefit.** The Trust Protector shall not exercise any power in a manner that would result in a direct or indirect financial benefit to the Trust Protector personally, except that a Trust Protector who is also a beneficiary may receive distributions otherwise authorized under the Trust.

**(b) No Revocation.** The Trust Protector shall not have the power to revoke this Trust or to make this Trust revocable.

**(c) Protection of Primary Beneficiaries.** The Trust Protector shall not change the identity of the primary beneficiaries or substantially reduce their interests without good cause, which shall include but not be limited to: protection from creditors, divorce, litigation, tax avoidance, or conduct detrimental to the Trust.

**(d) Fiduciary Duty.** The Trust Protector shall act as a fiduciary and shall exercise all powers in good faith and in the best interests of the Trust and its beneficiaries.

**(e) Good Faith Requirement.** All actions taken by the Trust Protector shall be taken in good faith and with reasonable care.

### Section 4. Resignation and Succession

**(a) Resignation.** A Trust Protector may resign by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the Trustee and, if reasonably practicable, to the adult beneficiaries.

**(b) Incapacity.** A Trust Protector shall be deemed to have resigned upon becoming legally incapacitated as determined by a court of competent jurisdiction or by written certification of two (2) licensed physicians.

**(c) Successor Appointment.** Upon resignation, removal, incapacity, or death of the Trust Protector, the Successor Trust Protector shall serve. If no Successor Trust Protector is designated or willing to serve, a successor may be appointed as provided in Section 1(d).

### Section 5. Compensation and Indemnification

**(a) Compensation.** The Trust Protector shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, payable from the Trust assets, in an amount comparable to fees charged by professional fiduciaries for similar services in the relevant jurisdiction.

**(b) Indemnification.** The Trust Protector shall be indemnified and held harmless from the Trust assets for any liability, cost, or expense incurred in the good faith performance of duties hereunder, except for liability arising from willful misconduct, gross negligence, or bad faith.

### Section 6. Exercise of Powers

**(a) Written Instrument.** Any power exercised by the Trust Protector shall be exercised by written instrument delivered to the Trustee and, where appropriate, recorded or filed as required by law.

**(b) No Duty to Act.** The Trust Protector shall have no duty to monitor the Trustee or the Trust and shall have no liability for failing to exercise any power granted herein.

**(c) Discretionary Nature.** All powers granted to the Trust Protector are discretionary, and the Trust Protector's decision to exercise or not exercise any power shall not be subject to review or challenge, except for actions taken in bad faith.
