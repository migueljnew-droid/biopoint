# BYLAWS
# OF
# ARX INFINITUM, LLC
## A Wyoming Private Trust Company

---

## PREAMBLE

These Bylaws are adopted to govern the internal affairs of ARX INFINITUM, LLC, a Wyoming limited liability company organized as a Private Trust Company pursuant to the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act, W.S. 17-29-101 et seq., and the Wyoming Private Trust Company Act, W.S. 17-23-101 et seq.

---

## ARTICLE I
## DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### Section 1.1. Definitions.

As used in these Bylaws, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

**(a) "Act"** means the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act, W.S. 17-29-101 et seq., as amended from time to time.

**(b) "Articles"** means the Articles of Organization of the Company as filed with the Wyoming Secretary of State, as amended from time to time.

**(c) "Board" or "Board of Directors"** means the governing body of the Company as constituted pursuant to Article III of these Bylaws.

**(d) "Company"** means ARX INFINITUM, LLC, a Wyoming limited liability company.

**(e) "Director"** means a member of the Board of Directors.

**(f) "Family Member"** means any person related by blood, marriage, or adoption to Miguel Jiminez, including lineal descendants, ancestors, siblings, and their respective spouses and lineal descendants.

**(g) "Fiduciary Capacity"** means service as trustee, co-trustee, agent, custodian, personal representative, guardian, conservator, or in any other capacity involving the management, administration, or disposition of property for the benefit of another.

**(h) "Managing Director"** means the chief executive officer of the Company appointed pursuant to Section 4.1 of these Bylaws.

**(i) "Member"** means a person who has been admitted as a member of the Company in accordance with the Operating Agreement.

**(j) "Operating Agreement"** means the Operating Agreement of the Company, as amended from time to time.

**(k) "Person"** means any individual, corporation, limited liability company, partnership, trust, estate, or other entity.

**(l) "Private Trust Company Act"** means the Wyoming Private Trust Company Act, W.S. 17-23-101 et seq., as amended from time to time.

**(m) "Trust"** means any trust for which the Company serves in a Fiduciary Capacity.

### Section 1.2. Interpretation.

(a) Unless the context otherwise requires, words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa.

(b) The headings in these Bylaws are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation hereof.

(c) References to Articles and Sections are to Articles and Sections of these Bylaws unless otherwise specified.

## ARTICLE II
## PURPOSE, POWERS, AND LIMITATIONS

### Section 2.1. Purpose.

The Company is organized and shall be operated exclusively as a Private Trust Company to provide fiduciary and trust administration services to Family Members and trusts established by or for the benefit of Family Members.

### Section 2.2. Powers.

Subject to the limitations set forth in Section 2.3, the Company shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out its purposes, including but not limited to the power to:

**(a)** Accept and administer trusts created by Family Members or for their benefit;

**(b)** Act as trustee, co-trustee, successor trustee, or agent under any trust instrument;

**(c)** Act as personal representative, executor, or administrator of estates;

**(d)** Act as guardian or conservator of the property of Family Members;

**(e)** Act as custodian under the Uniform Transfers to Minors Act or similar statutes;

**(f)** Receive, hold, invest, and manage property of every kind and description;

**(g)** Buy, sell, exchange, lease, and otherwise deal in real and personal property;

**(h)** Make loans and advances from trust funds in accordance with trust instruments and applicable law;

**(i)** Vote stock and exercise all rights of ownership in securities held in trust;

**(j)** Employ attorneys, accountants, investment advisors, and other professionals;

**(k)** Establish and maintain accounts at banks and other financial institutions;

**(l)** Execute and deliver all instruments necessary to carry out the Company's powers and purposes;

**(m)** Do all other acts necessary or incidental to the foregoing powers.

### Section 2.3. Limitations.

The Company shall NOT:

**(a)** Accept deposits from the general public;

**(b)** Make commercial loans or engage in banking activities;

**(c)** Act in a Fiduciary Capacity for any person who is not a Family Member or for any trust that was not established by or for the benefit of a Family Member;

**(d)** Solicit or advertise trust services to the general public;

**(e)** Issue shares or other securities to persons who are not Family Members;

**(f)** Engage in any activity that would require a banking license under Wyoming law.

### Section 2.4. Authorized Trusts.

The Company is specifically authorized to serve as trustee or in any other Fiduciary Capacity for the following trusts:

**(a)** OKLA DYNASTY TRUST (South Dakota perpetual dynasty trust);

**(b)** Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau Trust;

**(c)** Jiminez Private Family Foreign Situs Trust;

**(d)** National Redemption Outreach (religious assembly);

**(e)** Such other trusts as the Board may hereafter authorize in accordance with these Bylaws.

## ARTICLE III
## BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1. Powers and Responsibilities.

The business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by its Board of Directors. The Board shall have all powers necessary to govern the Company and to oversee its fiduciary activities, including:

**(a)** Establishing policies and procedures for trust administration;

**(b)** Reviewing and approving the acceptance of new trust appointments;

**(c)** Appointing and removing officers of the Company;

**(d)** Approving investment policies for trusts administered by the Company;

**(e)** Reviewing the performance of the Company and its officers;

**(f)** Approving annual budgets and financial statements;

**(g)** Ensuring compliance with applicable law and fiduciary standards;

**(h)** Amending these Bylaws as provided herein.

### Section 3.2. Number and Qualification.

**(a) Number.** The Board shall consist of not fewer than two (2) and not more than seven (7) Directors, as determined from time to time by resolution of the Board or by the Members.

**(b) Qualification.** Each Director shall be at least twenty-one (21) years of age. Directors need not be residents of Wyoming.

**(c) Family Requirement.** At least a majority of Directors shall be Family Members.

### Section 3.3. Initial Directors.

The initial Directors of the Company shall be:

**(a)** Miguel Jiminez (Managing Director)
**(b)** [Second Director to be designated]

### Section 3.4. Election and Term.

**(a) Election.** Directors shall be elected by the Members at the annual meeting of Members or at any special meeting called for that purpose.

**(b) Term.** Each Director shall hold office until the next annual meeting of Members and until a successor is elected and qualified, or until earlier resignation, removal, or death.

**(c) Perpetual Succession.** The Board structure shall provide for perpetual succession of the Company's fiduciary responsibilities regardless of changes in Board membership.

### Section 3.5. Vacancies.

**(a)** Any vacancy on the Board may be filled by a majority vote of the remaining Directors, even if less than a quorum, or by the Members.

**(b)** A Director elected to fill a vacancy shall serve for the unexpired term of the predecessor Director.

### Section 3.6. Removal.

**(a)** Any Director may be removed, with or without cause, by the Members.

**(b)** Any Director may be removed for cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the other Directors.

**(c)** Cause for removal includes, but is not limited to: breach of fiduciary duty, conviction of a crime involving dishonesty, gross negligence, or conduct that brings the Company into disrepute.

### Section 3.7. Resignation.

Any Director may resign at any time by giving written notice to the Managing Director or the Board. Such resignation shall take effect upon receipt or at such later time as specified therein.

### Section 3.8. Compensation.

Directors may receive reasonable compensation for their services as determined by the Board. Directors shall be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in connection with their duties.

## ARTICLE IV
## OFFICERS

### Section 4.1. Managing Director.

**(a) Appointment.** The Board shall appoint a Managing Director who shall serve as the chief executive officer of the Company.

**(b) Initial Appointment.** Miguel Jiminez is hereby appointed as the initial Managing Director of the Company.

**(c) Duties.** The Managing Director shall:

(i) Preside at all meetings of the Board and of the Members;

(ii) Have general supervision of the business and affairs of the Company;

(iii) Execute contracts, agreements, and other instruments on behalf of the Company;

(iv) Oversee the day-to-day administration of all trusts;

(v) Hire and supervise employees and agents of the Company;

(vi) Ensure compliance with applicable law and these Bylaws;

(vii) Report to the Board on the affairs of the Company;

(viii) Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the Board.

**(d) Term.** The Managing Director shall serve at the pleasure of the Board and may be removed at any time, with or without cause, by a majority vote of the Directors (excluding the Managing Director if applicable).

### Section 4.2. Secretary.

**(a)** The Board may appoint a Secretary who shall keep minutes of all meetings of the Board and the Members, maintain the Company's records, give notices required by law or these Bylaws, and perform such other duties as assigned by the Board or the Managing Director.

**(b)** In the absence of a Secretary, the Managing Director shall perform the duties of Secretary.

### Section 4.3. Treasurer.

**(a)** The Board may appoint a Treasurer who shall have custody of the Company's funds and securities, maintain financial records, prepare financial statements, and perform such other duties as assigned by the Board or the Managing Director.

**(b)** In the absence of a Treasurer, the Managing Director shall perform the duties of Treasurer.

### Section 4.4. Trust Officer.

**(a)** The Board may appoint one or more Trust Officers who shall be responsible for the administration of specific trusts or categories of trusts as designated by the Board or the Managing Director.

**(b)** Trust Officers shall have such duties and authority as may be delegated to them by the Board or the Managing Director.

### Section 4.5. Additional Officers.

The Board may appoint such additional officers as it deems necessary or appropriate, with such duties and authority as the Board may prescribe.

### Section 4.6. Removal of Officers.

Any officer may be removed at any time, with or without cause, by the Board.

### Section 4.7. Vacancies.

Any vacancy in any office may be filled by the Board.

## ARTICLE V
## MEETINGS

### Section 5.1. Annual Meeting of Directors.

**(a)** The Board shall hold an annual meeting for the transaction of such business as may properly come before the meeting.

**(b)** The annual meeting shall be held at such time and place as the Managing Director shall designate, but not later than ninety (90) days after the close of each fiscal year.

### Section 5.2. Regular Meetings.

**(a)** The Board shall hold regular meetings at least quarterly for the purpose of reviewing the Company's affairs and the trusts it administers.

**(b)** Regular meetings shall be held at such times and places as the Board may determine.

### Section 5.3. Special Meetings.

**(a)** Special meetings of the Board may be called by the Managing Director, or by any two (2) Directors.

**(b)** Notice of a special meeting shall be given to each Director at least forty-eight (48) hours prior to the meeting, stating the time, place, and purpose of the meeting.

### Section 5.4. Place of Meetings.

Meetings of the Board may be held at any place within or outside the State of Wyoming as designated in the notice of meeting.

### Section 5.5. Participation by Telephone or Electronic Means.

Directors may participate in any meeting by telephone, video conference, or other electronic means by which all participants can simultaneously communicate with each other. Such participation shall constitute presence at the meeting.

### Section 5.6. Quorum.

**(a)** A majority of the total number of Directors then in office shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

**(b)** If a quorum is not present, a majority of the Directors present may adjourn the meeting to another time without further notice.

### Section 5.7. Voting.

**(a)** Each Director shall have one vote.

**(b)** Except as otherwise required by these Bylaws or applicable law, the act of a majority of the Directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board.

### Section 5.8. Action Without a Meeting.

**(a)** Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if all Directors consent in writing to such action.

**(b)** Such written consent shall have the same force and effect as a unanimous vote of the Board.

**(c)** Consent may be given by electronic transmission.

### Section 5.9. Meeting Minutes.

**(a)** Minutes shall be prepared for each meeting of the Board and shall include:

(i) Date, time, and place of meeting;

(ii) Directors present and absent;

(iii) Summary of matters discussed;

(iv) Record of all votes and resolutions adopted;

(v) Signature of the Secretary or presiding officer.

**(b)** Minutes shall be maintained as part of the permanent records of the Company.

## ARTICLE VI
## TRUST ADMINISTRATION

### Section 6.1. Fiduciary Standards.

**(a)** In administering trusts, the Company shall act:

(i) Solely in the interest of the beneficiaries;

(ii) With the care, skill, prudence, and diligence that a prudent person acting in a like capacity would use;

(iii) In accordance with the terms of the governing trust instrument;

(iv) In compliance with applicable law.

**(b)** The Company shall avoid conflicts of interest and self-dealing except as expressly permitted by the trust instrument or applicable law.

### Section 6.2. Acceptance of Trusts.

**(a)** No trust shall be accepted by the Company without the prior approval of the Board or the Managing Director acting under authority delegated by the Board.

**(b)** Before accepting any trust, the Company shall review:

(i) The terms of the proposed trust instrument;

(ii) The nature and value of the trust property;

(iii) The identity of the settlor and beneficiaries;

(iv) The complexity and administrative requirements of the trust;

(v) Any potential conflicts of interest.

**(c)** The Company may decline to accept any trust for any reason consistent with its fiduciary duties.

### Section 6.3. Resignation as Trustee.

**(a)** The Company may resign as trustee of any trust in accordance with the terms of the trust instrument and applicable law.

**(b)** Prior to resignation, the Company shall give reasonable notice to the beneficiaries and shall cooperate in the appointment of a successor trustee.

### Section 6.4. Delegation of Investment Authority.

**(a)** The Company may delegate investment management authority to qualified investment advisors in accordance with prudent delegation standards.

**(b)** Such delegation shall be subject to the Company's ongoing supervision and periodic review.

### Section 6.5. Custody of Trust Property.

**(a)** Trust property shall be held in the name of the Company as trustee or in such other manner as may be appropriate.

**(b)** The Company shall maintain accurate records of all trust property and shall segregate trust property from the Company's own assets.

### Section 6.6. Trust Records.

**(a)** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate records for each trust, including:

(i) The trust instrument and any amendments;

(ii) Records of all receipts and disbursements;

(iii) Records of all investments and transactions;

(iv) Correspondence with beneficiaries and third parties;

(v) Tax returns and related documents;

(vi) Accountings rendered to beneficiaries.

**(b)** Trust records shall be retained for the duration of the trust and for at least seven (7) years thereafter, or longer if required by law.

### Section 6.7. Trust Accounting and Reporting.

**(a)** The Company shall provide periodic accountings to trust beneficiaries as required by the trust instrument or applicable law.

**(b)** At minimum, the Company shall provide an annual accounting to each adult current beneficiary.

**(c)** The Company shall file all required tax returns and reports for each trust.

### Section 6.8. Trust Fees.

**(a)** The Company may charge reasonable fees for its fiduciary services in accordance with fee schedules approved by the Board.

**(b)** Fees shall be disclosed to beneficiaries and shall be consistent with the trust instrument and applicable law.

## ARTICLE VII
## LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 7.1. Limitation of Liability.

**(a)** To the fullest extent permitted by the Act, no Director, officer, employee, or agent of the Company shall be personally liable to the Company or its Members for monetary damages for any act or omission in their capacity as such, except for:

(i) Breach of the duty of loyalty to the Company or its Members;

(ii) Acts or omissions not in good faith or involving intentional misconduct or knowing violation of law;

(iii) Transactions from which the person derived an improper personal benefit;

(iv) Willful or wanton misconduct.

**(b)** If the Act is amended to authorize further limitation of liability, the liability of Directors, officers, employees, and agents shall be limited to the fullest extent authorized by such amendment.

### Section 7.2. Indemnification.

**(a) Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify any person who was or is a party, or is threatened to be made a party, to any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative, by reason of the fact that such person is or was a Director, officer, employee, or agent of the Company, against expenses (including attorneys' fees), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred in connection with such action, suit, or proceeding, if such person acted in good faith and in a manner such person reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Company.

**(b) Criminal Proceedings.** In the case of any criminal action or proceeding, indemnification shall be provided only if such person had no reasonable cause to believe the conduct was unlawful.

**(c) Successful Defense.** To the extent that a Director, officer, employee, or agent has been successful on the merits or otherwise in defense of any action, suit, or proceeding, such person shall be indemnified against expenses (including attorneys' fees) actually and reasonably incurred.

### Section 7.3. Advancement of Expenses.

**(a)** Expenses incurred in defending any action, suit, or proceeding may be paid by the Company in advance of final disposition upon receipt of an undertaking by or on behalf of the person to repay such amount if it is ultimately determined that such person is not entitled to indemnification.

**(b)** The Board shall determine whether advancement of expenses is appropriate in each case.

### Section 7.4. Insurance.

The Company may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person who is or was a Director, officer, employee, or agent of the Company against any liability asserted against such person in such capacity, whether or not the Company would have the power to indemnify such person against such liability.

### Section 7.5. Non-Exclusivity.

The indemnification and advancement of expenses provided by this Article VII shall not be exclusive of any other rights to which a person may be entitled under any agreement, vote of Members, or otherwise.

## FISCAL MATTERS

## ARTICLE X
## AMENDMENTS

### Section 10.1. Amendment by Board.

These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Directors then in office at any regular or special meeting of the Board, provided that notice of the proposed amendment has been given to each Director at least ten (10) days prior to the meeting.

### Section 10.2. Amendment by Members.

These Bylaws may also be amended by the Members in accordance with the Operating Agreement.

### Section 10.3. Limitations.

No amendment shall be made that would:

**(a)** Permit the Company to engage in activities beyond the scope of a private trust company;

**(b)** Eliminate or reduce indemnification rights of Directors and officers except as permitted by law;

**(c)** Conflict with the Articles or the Act.

## ARTICLE XI
## MISCELLANEOUS

### Section 11.1. Governing Law.

These Bylaws shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming.

### Section 11.2. Severability.

If any provision of these Bylaws is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 11.3. Interpretation.

These Bylaws shall be interpreted in a manner consistent with the Articles and the Operating Agreement. In the event of any conflict, the Articles shall control, followed by the Operating Agreement, and then these Bylaws.

### Section 11.4. Notices.

**(a)** Any notice required or permitted to be given under these Bylaws shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when:

(i) Delivered personally;

(ii) Sent by facsimile or electronic mail, with confirmation of receipt;

(iii) One (1) business day after being sent by overnight courier;

(iv) Three (3) business days after being mailed by certified or registered mail, return receipt requested.

**(b)** Notices shall be sent to the address or electronic mail address of the recipient as shown on the records of the Company.

### Section 11.5. Waiver.

Any notice required by these Bylaws may be waived in writing by the person entitled to receive such notice, whether before, at, or after the time stated therein.
