## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the NASHOBA TRIBE OF SHABTAU TRUST (hereinafter "Trust"), EIN 98-1605207, was established as a 99-year tribal trust pursuant to a Trust Agreement dated _________________ (hereinafter "Original Trust Agreement");

**WHEREAS**, MIGUEL JIMINEZ currently serves as Trustee of the Trust;

**WHEREAS**, the Grantor/Settlor and/or authorized representative of the Trust desires to add provisions for a Trust Protector to enhance the flexibility, administration, and protection of the Trust, its assets, and the interests of the tribal community and beneficiaries served thereby;

**WHEREAS**, the Original Trust Agreement permits amendment thereof, or alternatively, the parties hereto possess the authority to amend the Trust pursuant to applicable law and the governing instruments of the tribal organization;

**WHEREAS**, this Amendment is intended to supplement and not replace the existing terms of the Original Trust Agreement, and all provisions of the Original Trust Agreement shall remain in full force and effect except as expressly modified herein;

**WHEREAS**, the Trust Protector provisions herein are designed to preserve the cultural heritage, mission, and long-term objectives of the Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau while providing modern trust administration flexibility;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Original Trust Agreement is hereby amended by the addition of the following Article:

---

**(c) Trust Protector Committee Option.** In recognition of the communal nature of tribal governance, a Trust Protector Committee may be designated in lieu of a single Trust Protector, consisting of not fewer than three (3) and not more than seven (7) individuals. If a Trust Protector Committee is designated:
   - (i) Members should, where practicable, include individuals with knowledge of and commitment to the cultural heritage and objectives of the Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau;
   - (ii) All powers granted herein shall be exercised by majority vote of the Committee members then serving;
   - (iii) The Committee may adopt reasonable procedures for meetings, notice, and decision-making.

**(d) Vacancy.** If no Trust Protector or Successor Trust Protector is serving or willing to serve:
   - (i) The adult beneficiaries or authorized tribal council members may appoint a successor Trust Protector by majority written consent; or
   - (ii) A court of competent jurisdiction may appoint a Trust Protector upon petition by an interested party.

**(e) Qualifications.** The Trust Protector should be an individual or entity with:
   - (i) Understanding of the mission and cultural objectives of the Trust;
   - (ii) No adverse interest to the Trust or its beneficiaries;
   - (iii) Ability to fulfill fiduciary obligations.

### Section 2. Powers of Trust Protector

The Trust Protector shall have the following powers, to be exercised in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of the Trust, the tribal community, and all beneficiaries:

**(a) Removal and Replacement of Trustee.** The Trust Protector may remove any Trustee, including any corporate Trustee such as ARX INFINITUM or its successors, with or without cause, and appoint a successor Trustee. In exercising this power, the Trust Protector shall consider the best interests of the Trust and the continuity of its tribal mission.

**(b) Appointment of Additional or Successor Trustees.** The Trust Protector may appoint successor Trustees or additional Co-Trustees as the Trust Protector deems appropriate for the proper administration of the Trust.

**(c) Modification of Administrative Provisions.** The Trust Protector may modify, amend, or supplement any administrative provisions of the Trust, provided that:
   - (i) Such modifications shall not alter the fundamental dispositive provisions or beneficial interests except as otherwise provided herein;
   - (ii) Such modifications shall be consistent with the cultural and community objectives of the Trust.

**(d) Change of Situs and Governing Law.** The Trust Protector may change the situs (principal place of administration) of the Trust and the governing law applicable to the Trust to any jurisdiction the Trust Protector deems to be in the best interests of the Trust, its beneficiaries, and the preservation of the tribal mission.

**(e) Decanting Authority.** The Trust Protector may distribute or appoint all or any portion of the Trust assets to a new trust (a "Recipient Trust") with terms substantially similar to those of this Trust, provided that:
   - (i) Such decanting does not reduce the interests of any current beneficiary;
   - (ii) The Recipient Trust maintains the tribal and community-focused objectives of this Trust;
   - (iii) Such decanting does not violate any express restriction in this Trust Agreement.

**(f) Addition or Removal of Beneficiaries.** The Trust Protector may add or remove beneficiaries from the class of eligible beneficiaries, provided that:
   - (i) Any added beneficiary shall be a member of the Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau, a descendant of a member, a spouse of a member, an affiliated organization serving the tribal community, or a charitable organization aligned with the Trust's mission;
   - (ii) Removal of a beneficiary shall only be for good cause, including but not limited to: threats of litigation against the Trust, creditor issues affecting Trust assets, conduct detrimental to the Trust or tribal community, or breach of tribal membership obligations.

**(g) Veto of Distributions.** The Trust Protector may veto any proposed distribution by the Trustee prior to such distribution being made. The Trustee shall provide reasonable notice to the Trust Protector of any intended discretionary distribution exceeding $_____________ [amount to be specified] or as otherwise directed by the Trust Protector.

**(h) Grantor/Non-Grantor Trust Conversion.** The Trust Protector may convert the Trust between grantor trust status and non-grantor trust status under the Internal Revenue Code, as the Trust Protector determines to be advantageous for the Trust, its tax position, and its beneficiaries.

**(i) Amendment for Law Changes.** The Trust Protector may amend the Trust to take advantage of changes in applicable tax law, trust law, tribal law, or other relevant statutes or regulations, provided such amendments are consistent with the original intent of the Trust as expressed in the Original Trust Agreement and preserve the tribal mission.

**(j) Termination Authority.** The Trust Protector may terminate the Trust if, in the Trust Protector's sole discretion, the continuation of the Trust has become impractical, uneconomical, or contrary to the best interests of the beneficiaries and the tribal community. Upon termination:
   - (i) Trust assets shall be distributed in accordance with the dispositive provisions of the Original Trust Agreement;
   - (ii) If those provisions are inapplicable, assets shall be distributed to benefit the tribal community or to a charitable organization with a similar mission.

**(k) Dispute Resolution.** The Trust Protector shall have the power to resolve disputes between Trustees and beneficiaries, among beneficiaries, or between the Trust and tribal governance bodies, concerning the interpretation or administration of the Trust. The Trust Protector's decision on such matters shall be final and binding on all parties.

**(l) Protection of Tribal Assets and Heritage.** The Trust Protector may take such actions as are necessary to protect tribal assets, cultural property, historical records, and the heritage interests of the Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau held within or associated with the Trust.

### Section 3. Limitations on Trust Protector Powers

**(a) No Self-Benefit.** The Trust Protector shall not exercise any power in a manner that would result in a direct or indirect financial benefit to the Trust Protector personally, except that a Trust Protector who is also a beneficiary may receive distributions otherwise authorized under the Trust.

**(b) No Revocation.** The Trust Protector shall not have the power to revoke this Trust or to make this Trust revocable.

**(c) Protection of Primary Beneficiaries and Tribal Mission.** The Trust Protector shall not:
   - (i) Change the identity of the primary beneficiaries or substantially reduce their interests without good cause;
   - (ii) Alter the fundamental tribal and community-focused mission of the Trust;
   - (iii) Dispose of cultural or heritage assets without consideration of their significance to the tribal community.

**(d) Fiduciary Duty.** The Trust Protector shall act as a fiduciary and shall exercise all powers in good faith and in the best interests of the Trust, its beneficiaries, and the tribal community.

**(e) Good Faith Requirement.** All actions taken by the Trust Protector shall be taken in good faith and with reasonable care.

**(f) Compliance with Tribal Governance.** Where applicable, the Trust Protector shall exercise powers in a manner consistent with any applicable tribal governance requirements or customs.

### Section 4. Resignation and Succession

**(a) Resignation.** A Trust Protector may resign by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the Trustee and, if reasonably practicable, to the adult beneficiaries and any tribal governance body.

**(b) Incapacity.** A Trust Protector shall be deemed to have resigned upon becoming legally incapacitated as determined by a court of competent jurisdiction or by written certification of two (2) licensed physicians.

**(c) Removal of Trust Protector.** A Trust Protector may be removed for cause (including breach of fiduciary duty, incapacity, or willful misconduct) by:
   - (i) Unanimous written consent of the adult beneficiaries; or
   - (ii) Majority vote of the tribal council or authorized governance body; or
   - (iii) Order of a court of competent jurisdiction.

**(d) Successor Appointment.** Upon resignation, removal, incapacity, or death of the Trust Protector, the Successor Trust Protector shall serve. If no Successor Trust Protector is designated or willing to serve, a successor may be appointed as provided in Section 1(d).

### Section 5. Compensation and Indemnification

**(a) Compensation.** The Trust Protector shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, payable from the Trust assets, in an amount comparable to fees charged by professional fiduciaries for similar services in the relevant jurisdiction.

**(b) Indemnification.** The Trust Protector shall be indemnified and held harmless from the Trust assets for any liability, cost, or expense incurred in the good faith performance of duties hereunder, except for liability arising from willful misconduct, gross negligence, or bad faith.

**(c) Advancement of Expenses.** The Trust may advance expenses to the Trust Protector for legal defense or other matters arising from the Trust Protector's service, subject to repayment if it is ultimately determined that the Trust Protector acted in bad faith or with willful misconduct.

### Section 6. Exercise of Powers

**(a) Written Instrument.** Any power exercised by the Trust Protector shall be exercised by written instrument delivered to the Trustee and, where appropriate, recorded or filed as required by law.

**(b) No Duty to Act.** The Trust Protector shall have no duty to monitor the Trustee or the Trust and shall have no liability for failing to exercise any power granted herein.

**(c) Discretionary Nature.** All powers granted to the Trust Protector are discretionary, and the Trust Protector's decision to exercise or not exercise any power shall not be subject to review or challenge, except for actions taken in bad faith.

**(d) Consultation.** The Trust Protector may, but shall not be required to, consult with beneficiaries, tribal elders, or other advisors before exercising any power.

### Section 7. Relationship to Tribal Governance

**(a) Coordination.** The Trust Protector shall coordinate with any applicable tribal governance structure in the administration of Trust Protector duties, while maintaining independent fiduciary judgment.

**(b) Preservation of Mission.** The Trust Protector shall at all times endeavor to preserve the cultural, educational, and community mission of the Nashoba Tribe of Shabtau Trust.
